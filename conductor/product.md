# Product Guide: Mathie

## Initial Concept
Mathie is an educational math game for Cambridge Primary Checkpoint (Grade 5) preparation. It features Flappy Bird-style gameplay with a Capybara character flying through math question gates.

## Target Audience
The primary users are Grade 5 students preparing for the Cambridge Primary Checkpoint exams.

## Core Goals
*   **Curriculum Mastery:** Provide comprehensive coverage of all Cambridge Grade 5 math strands to ensure exam readiness.
*   **High Engagement:** Utilize proven "Flappy Bird" style mechanics and a charming "Kawaii/Cottagecore" aesthetic to keep students motivated and practicing longer.
*   **Adaptive Learning:** Implement a personalized learning path using an Elo-based difficulty system and spaced repetition to target individual student weaknesses.

## Key Features
*   **Answer Gates:** An interactive 3-path gameplay mechanic where players must fly through the gate representing the correct answer to a math problem.
*   **Themed Worlds:** Progression through multiple environments (e.g., Forest, Garden, Sky) that correspond to specific Cambridge math strands.
*   **Reward & Customization System:**
    *   **Cosmetic Customization:** Players earn currency to unlock hats, fur patterns, and trail effects for their Capybara.
    *   **Garden Home Base:** A customizable space where players can place decorations unlocked through mathematical mastery.
    *   **Educational Sticker Book:** A collection of digital cards that provide visual explanations of math concepts.

## Curriculum Focus
The application covers all primary Cambridge Grade 5 math strands (Number, Geometry and Measure, Statistics and Probability), with the **delivery being dynamically adaptive to each student's performance** and identified weak areas.

## Desired Outcomes
*   **Academic Success:** Demonstrable improvement in Cambridge Primary Checkpoint test scores.
*   **Emotional Confidence:** A significant reduction in math-related anxiety through playful and rewarding practice.
*   **Independent Growth:** Development of autonomous learning habits where students take ownership of their mathematical progress.
